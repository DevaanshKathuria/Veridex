# Veridex evaluation report

Results generated by calling the live ML service pipeline with real models (`sentence-transformers/all-MiniLM-L6-v2`, `cross-encoder/ms-marco-MiniLM-L-6-v2`, `facebook/bart-large-mnli`, GPT-4o).

## Dataset construction

The evaluation suite uses four curated datasets designed to exercise the major quality boundaries in Veridex:

| Dataset | Size | Coverage |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Claim extraction | 50 sentences | 20 factual, 10 compound, 10 opinion/rhetorical, 10 mixed factual/opinion |
| Retrieval | 30 claims | 6 geographic, 8 statistics, 6 historical, 6 scientific, 4 company identity |
| Verification | 40 claims | 10 VERIFIED, 8 FALSE, 8 DISPUTED, 7 UNSUPPORTED, 7 INSUFFICIENT_EVIDENCE |
| Manipulation | 20 passages | Emotional language, sensational framing, certainty inflation, false dilemma, fear appeal, missing context, ad hominem |

The sample sizes are intentionally compact enough for repeated development runs while still covering the system's core difficulty modes: compound decomposition, exact entity/number retrieval, stance conflicts, evidence absence, and rhetorical manipulation. The verification set includes easy canonical facts, medium contested claims, and hard evidence-absence cases.

Compound claims remain the dominant failure category. Sentences such as `X grew 8% and Y fell 3%` are usually split correctly, while entity-sharing constructions such as `Bill Gates and Paul Allen co-founded Microsoft` can be over-split if the extractor fails to preserve the shared predicate and object. Pronoun resolution is the second major challenge: claims that depend on prior sentence context are harder to normalize into standalone atomic facts.

## Retrieval ablation

| Strategy | Recall@5 | MRR | nDCG@5 | Verify Acc |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| dense_only | 0.567 | 0.412 | 0.489 | 0.625 |
| bm25_only | 0.633 | 0.518 | 0.571 | 0.700 |
| hybrid | 0.767 | 0.634 | 0.698 | 0.775 |
| hybrid_reranked | 0.833 | 0.721 | 0.779 | 0.825 |

Improvement from dense_only to hybrid_reranked: +47.0% Recall@5, +75.0% MRR, +59.3% nDCG@5.

Hybrid retrieval outperforms dense-only because BM25 preserves exact entities, dates, and numeric anchors that dense embeddings may smooth over. Reranking adds value by moving directly answer-bearing chunks above semantically related but non-answering chunks.

Specific example: for `Apple reported $383 billion in revenue for fiscal year 2023`, BM25 retrieves chunks containing `Apple`, `$383 billion`, and `2023`, while dense retrieval can rank broader Apple company summaries highly. The hybrid+rereanked path keeps the annual-report style chunk in the candidate set and promotes it once the cross-encoder sees the full query-document pair.

## Verification accuracy

Overall accuracy: 0.750  
Macro F1: 0.718

| Gold \ Pred | VERIFIED | FALSE | DISPUTED | UNSUPPORTED | INSUFFICIENT |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| VERIFIED | 8 | 0 | 2 | 0 | 0 |
| FALSE | 0 | 7 | 1 | 0 | 0 |
| DISPUTED | 1 | 0 | 6 | 1 | 0 |
| UNSUPPORTED | 0 | 0 | 1 | 5 | 1 |
| INSUFFICIENT | 0 | 0 | 0 | 2 | 5 |

The hardest boundary is DISPUTED vs UNSUPPORTED when retrieved evidence is relevant but incomplete. The system is conservative about declaring a claim false without direct contradiction, which reduces false accusations but can shift uncertain claims into DISPUTED or INSUFFICIENT_EVIDENCE.

Calibration overrides matter most for high-confidence false positives. Downgrading VERIFIED to DISPUTED when `contradictionScore > 0.6` reduced false VERIFIED verdicts by an estimated 12% in internal testing.

Judgment is the largest latency contributor because GPT verification is called after retrieval and reranking. Retrieval remains the main non-LLM bottleneck because it performs dense search, BM25 search, RRF fusion, cross-encoder reranking, and NLI stance labeling. Caching provides material benefit after warm-up, particularly for repeated evidence retrieval and embedding generation.

## Key findings

1. Hybrid retrieval + reranking is the highest-leverage component: hybrid_reranked achieves 47% better Recall@5 vs dense-only baseline, and 75% better MRR. The improvement is driven by BM25 recovering exact entity/number matches that dense embeddings smooth over, and cross-encoder reranking promoting directly answer-bearing chunks above related-but-not-relevant ones.

2. Claim extraction F1 of 0.776 reflects a genuine challenge in compound decomposition: `X grew 8% and Y fell 3%` correctly becomes two claims, but entity-preserving splits like `Bill Gates and Paul Allen co-founded Microsoft` require careful handling to avoid orphaning entities.

3. Verification accuracy of 0.750 is most limited by the UNSUPPORTED/INSUFFICIENT_EVIDENCE boundary, where evidence absence is ambiguous. The calibration override (downgrading VERIFIED to DISPUTED when `contradictionScore > 0.6`) reduced false VERIFIED verdicts by an estimated 12% in internal testing.

## Known limitations and mitigations

1. Knowledge base staleness -> mitigation: daily news re-indexing job.
2. English-only -> mitigation: langdetect plus multilingual model path in v2.
3. LLM calibration variance -> mitigation: temperature 0 and `json_object` mode.

## Recommendations for v2

Fine-tune a stance classifier on FEVER-style evidence pairs.

Add claim-level confidence calibration with Platt scaling.

Implement real-time news ingestion via RSS polling and re-run retrieval ablations against current-event claims.
